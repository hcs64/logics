Modal logics, temporal and dynamic

Overview

This report deals with a branch of formal logic called Propositional Modal Logicin which we are concerned with the truth of propositions.
A proposition is a statement that is considered to be true or false, such as:

  1. "It is raining."
  2. "The sky is green."
  3. "If the sky is green, it will never rain again."
  4. "It could rain or it could snow, maybe both."
Some propositions will be considered atomic, meaning indivisible, because we will not consider them to be made out of simpler concepts. We could analyze "it is raining" as a statement about weather, at some time, and regarding some weather condition, or we could ignore those details if we are only interested in the truth of the overall statement. Propositions 1 and 2 above can be treated in this way.
