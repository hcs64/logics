Here I am going to host sections of a report on the branch of formal logic called modal logic. It is hard to tell what shape this will take so far, but I intend to deal mostly with dynamic logics (propositional and first order), with some discussion of linear time temporal logic and possibly autoepistemic logic.

This will also serve as a playground for me to experiment with MathJax and active web content in general. Hopefully it will be usable and readable but that is not the primary goal, or so I tell myself.

A brief explanation of

Modal logic

Very basic formal logics deal with a single state of the universe. A proposition is true, or it is false. There is no sense of what it has been, or what it could be, or how it might get to be that way. A natural way to understand modal logic is that it allows us to work with alternate universes.
